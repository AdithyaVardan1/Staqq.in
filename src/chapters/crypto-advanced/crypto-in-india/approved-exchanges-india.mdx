# Approved Exchanges in India

Choosing the right exchange is the first practical decision for any Indian crypto investor. Not all platforms are equal in terms of compliance, security, or product offering.

  Educational content only. Exchange listings and compliance status can change. Always verify current FIU-IND registration status before depositing funds. This is not an endorsement of any exchange. Not financial advice.
</InfoBox>

## What Makes an Exchange "Approved" in India

There is no formal "approved" list published by the government. However, exchanges operating legally in India must:

1. Be registered with **FIU-IND** under PMLA
2. Comply with **KYC/AML** requirements (verify user identity)
3. Report suspicious transactions
4. Maintain transaction records

## How to Verify an Exchange

1. Check if the exchange is listed on the **FIU-IND website** (fiu.gov.in)
2. Look for KYC requirements — a legitimate exchange will always ask for PAN, Aadhaar, and bank details
3. Check for Indian banking support (IMPS/NEFT/UPI) for INR deposits
4. Review the exchange's security history (has it been hacked? How much was lost?)

## Self-Custody vs Exchange Custody

<Table
  headers={["Option", "Pros", "Cons"]}
  rows={[
    ["Exchange wallet", "Convenient; no key management", "Exchange holds your funds; counterparty risk (hacks, insolvency)"],
    ["Self-custody (hardware wallet)", "Full control; no counterparty risk", "You are responsible for key security; no recovery if lost"],
  ]}
/>

A common strategy: trade on exchange, then move large holdings to a hardware wallet (Ledger, Trezor) for long-term storage.

<InfoBox type="warning">
  "Not your keys, not your coins." Several large exchanges globally have failed (FTX, Celsius, Voyager), wiping out user funds. For holdings beyond short-term trading, consider moving to self-custody. The FTX collapse in November 2022 cost users an estimated $8 billion.
</InfoBox>

## Key Takeaways

<InfoBox type="tip">
  - Use only FIU-IND registered, PMLA-compliant exchanges in India
  - Major Indian options: CoinDCX, WazirX, Zebpay, CoinSwitch, BitBNS
  - Global options with Indian presence: Binance (re-registered 2024), KuCoin, OKX
