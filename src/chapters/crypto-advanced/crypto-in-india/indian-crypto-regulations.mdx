# Indian Crypto Regulations

India has one of the world's largest crypto user bases but has taken a cautious regulatory approach. Understanding the current legal landscape is essential before investing.

<InfoBox type="important">
  Educational content only. This reflects the regulatory environment as of early 2025. Laws can change. Consult a qualified legal or financial adviser for advice specific to your situation. This is not legal advice.
</InfoBox>

## Is Crypto Legal in India?

**Yes — but heavily regulated.**

Crypto is not banned in India. The Supreme Court lifted the RBI's banking ban in March 2020. However, it is also not recognised as legal tender. The government has chosen to tax and regulate it rather than ban it.

Key government positions:
- Crypto is classified as a **Virtual Digital Asset (VDA)** under the Finance Act 2022
- The RBI has expressed concerns about financial stability but has not banned it
- The government has not passed a comprehensive Crypto Bill as of early 2025 — regulation happens through tax law and PMLA rules

## The PMLA Framework

In March 2023, crypto exchanges and intermediaries in India were brought under the **Prevention of Money Laundering Act (PMLA)**.

This means:
- Crypto exchanges must register with **FIU-IND** (Financial Intelligence Unit of India)
- Mandatory KYC (Know Your Customer) for all users
- Suspicious transaction reporting obligations
- Record-keeping requirements

Any platform offering crypto trading, transfer, custody, or exchange services must be PMLA-compliant and registered with FIU-IND.

<InfoBox type="note">
  Binance was delisted by FIU-IND in late 2023 for non-compliance with PMLA rules. It later re-registered in 2024. This showed that India is serious about enforcing AML rules on crypto platforms.
</InfoBox>

## SEBI's Role

As of 2024, **SEBI (Securities and Exchange Board of India)** was exploring oversight of crypto assets that function like securities. A multi-regulator framework is being considered:

- SEBI for crypto assets resembling securities
- RBI for stablecoins and payment-adjacent tokens
- A potential new body for pure cryptocurrencies like BTC and ETH

No final framework has been announced, but SEBI has conducted public consultations and is actively working toward regulation.

## RBI's CBDC: The Digital Rupee

The Reserve Bank of India launched a **Central Bank Digital Currency (CBDC)** called the **Digital Rupee (e-₹)** in 2022–2023.

- Unlike decentralised crypto, the Digital Rupee is issued and controlled by the RBI
- It is legal tender and carries the same value as physical rupee notes
- Designed for wholesale (bank-to-bank) and retail use cases
- Available through select banks including SBI, HDFC, ICICI, Kotak

The Digital Rupee is separate from private cryptocurrencies and is not traded on exchanges.

## What May Change

- A comprehensive **Virtual Digital Assets Bill** has been discussed but not passed
- India's G20 presidency in 2023 pushed for a global crypto regulatory framework
- FATF (Financial Action Task Force) recommendations may shape future AML rules
- Further clarity on stablecoin regulation is expected

<InfoBox type="warning">
  Regulatory risk is a real factor for Indian crypto investors. A future law could impose new restrictions, require exchanges to delist certain tokens, or introduce new compliance requirements. Staying informed about regulatory developments is part of responsible investing.
</InfoBox>

## Key Takeaways

<InfoBox type="tip">
  - Crypto is legal in India but classified as a Virtual Digital Asset (VDA), not legal tender
  - Exchanges must be registered with FIU-IND and follow PMLA/KYC rules
  - SEBI is exploring oversight of crypto assets resembling securities
  - The RBI's Digital Rupee is India's official CBDC — separate from private crypto
  - A comprehensive crypto bill has not been passed; regulation is still evolving
  - Always use registered, PMLA-compliant platforms when trading in India
</InfoBox>
