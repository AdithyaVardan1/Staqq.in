# Crypto Taxation in India

India introduced one of the world's most stringent crypto tax regimes in Budget 2022. Understanding these rules is essential for every Indian crypto investor.

<InfoBox type="important">
  Educational content only. Tax laws are complex and subject to change. This reflects rules as of Budget 2024. Consult a qualified Chartered Accountant (CA) for advice on your specific situation. This is not tax advice.
</InfoBox>

## The Core Rules (Introduced in Budget 2022)

### 30% Flat Tax on VDA Gains

**All profits from Virtual Digital Assets (VDAs) — including crypto and NFTs — are taxed at a flat 30% rate.**

- No distinction between short-term and long-term gains
- No benefit of the basic exemption limit (the ₹3L threshold does not reduce VDA tax)
- Applicable from FY 2022-23 (Assessment Year 2023-24) onwards
- Plus 4% cess = effective rate of **31.2%**

**Example:**
- Buy ETH at ₹1,00,000
- Sell at ₹1,50,000
- Gain = ₹50,000
- Tax = ₹50,000 × 30% = ₹15,000 + 4% cess = **₹15,600**

### No Loss Set-Off or Carry Forward

This is the most painful rule:

- **You cannot offset crypto losses against gains from other crypto assets**
- **You cannot offset crypto losses against any other income (salary, stocks, etc.)**
- **You cannot carry forward losses to future years**

**Example:**
- BTC trade: +₹1,00,000 profit
- ETH trade: -₹80,000 loss
- You still pay 30% on ₹1,00,000 = ₹31,200 tax. The ₹80,000 loss is irrelevant.

<InfoBox type="warning">
  This no-loss-offset rule is uniquely harsh compared to equity taxation in India, where losses can be set off against gains of the same type and carried forward for 8 years. Crypto traders need to be acutely aware of this when planning their trades.
</InfoBox>

### 1% TDS on Crypto Transactions

**From July 1, 2022, a 1% Tax Deducted at Source (TDS) applies to every crypto transaction above ₹10,000 per year (₹50,000 for specified persons).**

- Applies to buyer: when you buy crypto, 1% of the purchase amount is deducted and paid to the government
- On Indian exchanges: the exchange handles TDS deduction automatically
- On P2P or foreign platforms: the buyer must deduct and deposit TDS — complex compliance

**Purpose:** This creates a transaction trail so the government can identify and track crypto activity.

## What Counts as a Taxable Event?

- Selling crypto for INR
- Trading one crypto for another (crypto-to-crypto swaps are taxable)
- Using crypto to buy goods or services
- Receiving crypto as payment (taxed as income at full value on receipt date)
- NFT sales

## What Is Not a Taxable Event

- Transferring crypto between your own wallets
- Buying crypto (no tax at time of purchase)
- Unrealised gains (holding crypto that has gone up in value but not sold)

## Gifts and Inheritance

- **Receiving crypto as a gift:** Taxed as "income from other sources" at your slab rate if received from non-relatives and value exceeds ₹50,000 in a year
- **Giving crypto as a gift:** The giver has no immediate tax, but the recipient's cost of acquisition is the fair market value on the date of gift
- Gifts from relatives (as defined under the Income Tax Act) are exempt

## Reporting Requirements

Indian crypto investors must report their holdings and gains in the **Income Tax Return (ITR)**.

Schedule VDA was added to ITR forms specifically for this purpose. You must report:
- Date of acquisition
- Date of transfer
- Purchase price (cost of acquisition)
- Sale price
- Gain/loss per transaction

<InfoBox type="note">
  Indian exchanges like Zebpay, CoinDCX, and WazirX provide transaction history exports that can help compile your tax records. Third-party tools like Koinly and ClearTax also support crypto tax calculation for Indian users.
</InfoBox>

## The TDS Refund Mechanism

If TDS deducted exceeds your actual tax liability, you can claim a refund when filing your ITR. This is particularly relevant if you have low total income.

## Key Takeaways

<InfoBox type="tip">
  - All VDA gains taxed at flat 30% + 4% cess = 31.2% effective rate
  - No loss set-off: crypto losses cannot offset other crypto gains or any other income
  - 1% TDS on every transaction above threshold — exchanges handle this automatically
  - Crypto-to-crypto swaps are taxable events in India
  - Report all transactions in Schedule VDA of your ITR
  - Consult a CA familiar with crypto taxation for complex situations
</InfoBox>
