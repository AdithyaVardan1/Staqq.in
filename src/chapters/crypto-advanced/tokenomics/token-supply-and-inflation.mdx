# Token Supply & Inflation

Token supply is one of the most overlooked aspects of crypto investing. Two tokens with the same price can have wildly different actual values based on their supply design.

**Fully Diluted Valuation (FDV) = Price × Max Supply** — tells you what the whole project is valued at if all tokens were in circulation.

## Deflationary vs Inflationary Tokens

**Deflationary (hard cap):**
- Bitcoin: 21 million max supply, decreasing issuance through halvings
- As supply growth approaches zero, price is determined purely by demand
- Scarcity narrative drives long-term value thesis

**Inflationary (no hard cap):**
- Ethereum: New ETH is issued as staking rewards, but ETH is also burned via EIP-1559
- Most proof-of-stake tokens have perpetual inflation to pay validators
- Net inflation vs deflation depends on fee burn vs staking issuance

**Hyperinflationary (ponzi warning):**
- Many DeFi tokens issue billions of tokens as yield rewards
- Rapid supply expansion dilutes existing holders unless demand grows proportionally
- Token price often trends toward zero as emissions continue

## Bitcoin Halvings

Every ~4 years, Bitcoin's block reward (new BTC issued per block) is cut in half.

<Table
  headers={["Year", "Block Reward", "New BTC/Year (approx)"]}

    ["2016", "12.5 BTC", "657,000"],
    ["2020", "6.25 BTC", "328,500"],
    ["2024", "3.125 BTC", "164,250"],
    ["~2028", "1.5625 BTC", "~82,000"],

/>

By 2140, all 21 million BTC will have been mined. After that, miners are compensated purely by transaction fees.

<InfoBox type="note">
  Halvings reduce the rate at which new supply enters the market. Historically, halvings have preceded significant bull markets — though correlation isn't causation, and past performance doesn't predict future results.
</InfoBox>

## Ethereum's Supply Model (Post-Merge)

Ethereum now issues new ETH as staking rewards (~0.5–0.8% annual issuance) BUT also burns a base fee on every transaction (EIP-1559).

When network activity is high enough, burns exceed issuance — making ETH net deflationary.

## Key Takeaways

<InfoBox type="tip">
  - Circulating supply ≠ total supply ≠ max supply; understand all three
  - Deflationary tokens (BTC) get scarcer over time; most PoS tokens have ongoing inflation
  - Bitcoin halvings reduce new issuance every ~4 years, approaching zero by 2140
  - Ethereum's EIP-1559 burn can make ETH net-deflationary during high activity
  - High FDV vs market cap = large future dilution — a red flag for newer tokens
</InfoBox>
