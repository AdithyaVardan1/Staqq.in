# Vesting & Distribution

How a project allocates its tokens and when those allocations unlock reveals the alignment (or misalignment) of incentives between founders, investors, and the community.

## What is a Vesting Schedule?

Vesting prevents insiders from dumping all tokens immediately at launch. Instead, tokens unlock gradually over time.

**Common vesting structures:**

**Cliff + Linear Vesting:**
- 1-year cliff: no tokens released for the first year
- Then linear release: e.g., 25% per year for 3 more years

**Example:** 
- 10 million tokens for team
- 1-year cliff, 3-year linear vesting
- Month 12: 0 tokens released
- Month 13: 277,778 tokens/month begin releasing
- Month 48: All 10M tokens fully vested

## Why Vesting Matters

Without vesting, founders and VCs could sell everything on day 1 — classic "pump and dump."

With proper vesting:
- Insiders are incentivised to build long-term value
- Market isn't flooded with supply at launch
- Token holders know when future supply will enter the market

<InfoBox type="warning">
  **Token unlock events** are predictable selling pressure. When a large tranche of tokens unlocks for team or VCs, some holders will sell. Track upcoming unlocks at **token.unlocks.app** or **vestlab.io**. A major unlock event can precede significant price drops.
</InfoBox>

## Red Flags in Token Distribution

- **Team allocation > 30%** — founders can dump and abandon project
- **No vesting or very short cliff** — insiders can exit quickly
- **Opaque allocation** — percentages not disclosed in documentation
- **Large early investor allocation with short vesting** — VCs may be selling to retail
- **Very low community allocation** — project isn't genuinely decentralised

## Green Flags

- **Team tokens locked 3–4 years with meaningful cliff**
- **Community allocation >30%**
- **Multi-sig or DAO-controlled treasury**
- **Transparent vesting schedule published and verifiable on-chain**

## Reading a Tokenomics Pie Chart

When you see a tokenomics pie chart, ask:
1. What is the team/investor % combined?
2. What is the vesting schedule for those tokens?
3. When does the largest unlock event happen?
4. Is the treasury controlled by a DAO or by the company?

## Key Takeaways

<InfoBox type="tip">
  - Token distribution reveals who benefits most and whether incentives are aligned
  - Vesting schedules prevent insiders from dumping at launch
  - Cliff + linear vesting is the industry standard: no tokens for X months, then gradual release
  - Track upcoming unlock events — predictable selling pressure
  - Red flags: high team/VC % with short vesting; opaque allocations; tiny community share
</InfoBox>
