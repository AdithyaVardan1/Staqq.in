# Diversification in Crypto

Diversification in crypto is not the same as in traditional investing. Assets that appear to be different often move together — especially during market crashes.

<InfoBox type="important">
  Educational content only. Diversification does not guarantee against loss. Crypto markets are highly volatile and correlated during downturns. Not financial advice.
</InfoBox>

## The Correlation Problem

In traditional investing, diversification works because different assets move differently. Bonds often rise when stocks fall. Gold tends to hold value in recessions.

In crypto:
- During bull markets, most altcoins rise together (everything goes up)
- During bear markets, most altcoins fall together — often harder than Bitcoin
- During systemic crises (Luna 2022, FTX 2022), even "safe" assets like stablecoins lost pegs

**Holding 10 altcoins is not the same as being diversified** if they all move in the same direction.

<InfoBox type="note">
  In May 2022, Luna/UST crashed 99.9% in 72 hours. It dragged the entire crypto market down with it. BTC fell from ~$40k to $28k. ETH fell from ~$2,800 to $1,800. Correlation spiked to near 1.0 across the board.
</InfoBox>

### By Sector

Like stocks, crypto has sectors:

- **Layer 1s** (blockchains): ETH, SOL, AVAX, NEAR
- **Layer 2s** (scaling): ARB, OP, Polygon
- **DeFi**: UNI, AAVE, MKR, CRV
- **NFT/Gaming**: sector highly speculative, historically boom-bust
- **RWA (Real-World Assets)**: tokenised bonds, property — emerging sector
- **AI-crypto intersection**: FET, RENDER, TAO — narrative-driven

Holding across sectors reduces the risk of a single sector crash wiping your portfolio.

### By Holding Duration

- **Long-term core:** BTC and ETH make up a large base you hold through market cycles
- **Medium-term positions:** established altcoins held for months
- **Short-term / speculative:** small positions in higher-risk tokens with defined exit targets

## What Diversification Does NOT Protect Against

- **Market-wide crypto crashes** — when BTC corrects 50–80%, most altcoins fall further
- **Regulatory risk** — a ban or major regulatory action affects all crypto at once
- **Stablecoin de-pegging** — your USDC or USDT might not be worth $1 in a crisis
- **Exchange collapse** — if you hold on an exchange and it fails, diversification across tokens on that exchange doesn't help

## Practical Approach

A simple framework for a first crypto portfolio:

1. **Establish a core (50–70%):** BTC and/or ETH — the most proven assets
2. **Add some established altcoins (20–30%):** Tokens with real usage, established communities, and multi-year track records
3. **Limit speculative positions (10–20%):** Higher-risk bets with money you can afford to lose entirely
4. **Hold some stablecoins:** Useful for buying dips or as a buffer during volatility

## Key Takeaways

<InfoBox type="tip">
  - Crypto diversification is limited — most assets are highly correlated during crashes
  - Diversify by asset class (BTC, ETH, altcoins, stablecoins), sector, and time horizon
  - Start with a large-cap core (BTC/ETH), add mid-caps carefully, keep speculative positions small
  - Crypto allocation within a total portfolio should reflect your overall risk tolerance
  - Diversification within crypto does not protect against market-wide crashes or exchange failures
</InfoBox>
