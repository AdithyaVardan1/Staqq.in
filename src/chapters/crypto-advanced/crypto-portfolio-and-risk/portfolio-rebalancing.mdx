# Portfolio Rebalancing

Rebalancing means periodically adjusting your portfolio back to your target allocation. In crypto — where assets can move 10x or lose 90% — this discipline matters more than in most asset classes.

<InfoBox type="important">
  Educational content only. Rebalancing strategies involve real costs including taxes and trading fees. Not financial advice.
</InfoBox>

## Why Rebalancing Matters in Crypto

Imagine you start with:
- 50% BTC
- 30% ETH
- 20% altcoins

After a bull run, ETH has tripled while BTC doubled and altcoins quintupled. Your portfolio is now:
- 35% BTC
- 32% ETH
- 33% altcoins

Your risk profile has shifted — you're now much more exposed to volatile altcoins than you intended. Without rebalancing, your portfolio drifts toward your best-performing (and often riskiest) assets at the peak of their runs.

## The Core Benefit: Sell High, Buy Low Systematically

Rebalancing forces you to:
- **Trim positions that have grown to be overweight** (selling some of your winners)
- **Add to positions that have fallen below target** (buying assets that are cheaper than they were)

This is the mechanical implementation of "buy low, sell high" — without requiring you to perfectly time the market.

## Rebalancing Methods

### Calendar Rebalancing

Set a fixed schedule (monthly, quarterly, annually) and rebalance regardless of price.

- **Pros:** Simple, disciplined, no market-timing required
- **Cons:** You may rebalance at an inconvenient time; misses major moves within the period

### Threshold Rebalancing

Rebalance when any asset drifts more than X% from its target.

- Example: Rebalance when any holding moves more than 5% away from its target allocation
- **Pros:** More responsive to actual market moves
- **Cons:** Can trigger frequent trades in volatile markets; more monitoring required

### Hybrid Approach

Rebalance on a schedule AND whenever thresholds are breached.

## The Crypto Tax Problem with Rebalancing

In India, every rebalancing trade is a taxable event:
- Selling BTC to buy ETH = taxable sale of BTC
- All gains taxed at 30% flat + 4% cess
- Losses from rebalancing cannot offset gains elsewhere

**This significantly changes the math.** A rebalancing trade that generates a ₹1,00,000 gain costs ₹31,200 in tax. You need to decide whether the risk-management benefit outweighs the tax cost.

<InfoBox type="warning">
  For Indian crypto investors, rebalancing has a higher tax cost than in most markets due to the flat 30% rate and no loss offset. Factor tax implications into every rebalancing decision. Consider consulting a CA before rebalancing large positions.
</InfoBox>

## Practical Rebalancing Strategies

### New Money Rebalancing

Instead of selling overweight positions, direct new investments into underweight positions. No tax triggered.

**Example:** If altcoins are underweight, put your next month's investment entirely into altcoins until the balance is restored.

### Partial Rebalancing

Instead of returning exactly to target, bring positions back to within 5% of target. Reduces trade count and tax events.

### Stablecoin Buffer Rebalancing

Hold 10–15% in stablecoins permanently. When an asset falls significantly, deploy stablecoin buffer into it. When an asset pumps, take some profit back into stablecoins. This smooths rebalancing without triggering crypto-to-crypto trades.

## Sample Rebalancing Workflow

1. Check portfolio allocations monthly
2. Identify which assets are >5% above or below target
3. Estimate tax cost of any required sale
4. If rebalancing benefit (risk reduction) > tax cost: execute
5. Consider using new money or stablecoin buffer first
6. Record all trades accurately for ITR Schedule VDA filing

## What Not to Rebalance

- **Do not rebalance based on short-term price moves** — crypto's daily 5–10% swings should not trigger a rebalance
- **Do not over-trade** — frequent small rebalances create tax events and fees that erode returns
- **Do not sell a core position (BTC/ETH) to chase altcoin performance** at the top of a rally

## Key Takeaways

<InfoBox type="tip">
  - Rebalancing maintains your intended risk profile as prices move
  - Calendar or threshold-based approaches both work; choose the one you'll actually follow
  - In India, every crypto-to-crypto sale is a taxable event at 30% flat — factor this in
  - Use new money, stablecoin buffers, or partial rebalancing to minimise tax impact
  - Do not rebalance in response to daily volatility — set meaningful thresholds
</InfoBox>
