# Avoiding Scams

Crypto attracts more scams than almost any other financial sector. The combination of irreversible transactions, pseudonymity, and get-rich-quick narratives makes it a target-rich environment for fraud.

<InfoBox type="important">
  Educational content only. If you suspect fraud, report it to CERT-In (cert-in.org.in), cybercrime.gov.in, or your local cyber cell. This is not legal advice.
</InfoBox>

## Why Crypto Scams Are So Effective

- Transactions are **irreversible** — once sent, crypto cannot be recalled
- Pseudonymous addresses make it hard to trace scammers
- Global reach — scammers can target anyone anywhere
- Many victims are new to crypto and don't know what "normal" looks like
- The technology is complex enough that social engineering works

## The Most Common Crypto Scams

### Fake Giveaway Scams

"Send 1 ETH, get 2 back." Elon Musk, Vitalik Buterin, and other famous names are routinely impersonated on social media for these scams.

**Rule:** No legitimate project, celebrity, or exchange ever asks you to send crypto first to receive more back. Ever.

### Rug Pulls

A new token launches with heavy promotion. Developers hype it, liquidity pours in, price pumps. Then developers drain the liquidity pool and disappear with the funds.

**Warning signs:**
- Anonymous team with no verifiable identity
- No audit of smart contracts
- Extremely high early returns with no clear mechanism
- Locked liquidity with a very short unlock timeframe
- Social media channels where critical questions are deleted

### Phishing

Fake websites that look identical to real exchanges or wallets. You enter your seed phrase or password — they steal it.

**Protection:**
- Bookmark real exchange URLs; never search for an exchange name and click the first result
- The only time you enter your seed phrase is during wallet setup on your own device — never anywhere else
- Check URLs character by character (coinbase.com vs. c0inbase.com)

### Romance Scams ("Pig Butchering" / Sha Zhu Pan)

A scammer builds a relationship over weeks or months via social media or dating apps. They earn your trust, then introduce you to a "great crypto investment opportunity" — often a fake exchange they control. You deposit money, it appears to grow, you deposit more. When you try to withdraw, they vanish.

This is one of the most financially devastating scam types. Victims lose tens of thousands to crores of rupees.

**Rule:** Be very skeptical of any romantic connection that steers the conversation toward crypto investments.

### Pump and Dump

A low-liquidity token is aggressively promoted (often in Telegram/WhatsApp groups). Retail buyers push the price up. The promoters (who bought early) sell at the top, crashing the price.

### Fake ICOs / Token Sales

Projects raising money through token sales without delivering any product. The team takes investor funds and disappears.

### Clipboard Hijacking Malware

Malware that monitors your clipboard. When you copy a wallet address to paste, it replaces it with the attacker's address. You send your crypto to the wrong address.

**Protection:** Always verify the first and last 4 characters of a wallet address after pasting.

## How to Protect Yourself

1. **Never share your seed phrase or private key** — with anyone, ever, for any reason
2. **Use hardware wallets** for significant holdings
3. **Use 2FA** (preferably app-based, not SMS) on all exchange accounts
4. **Bookmark exchanges** — never type or search for URLs
5. **Verify addresses** character by character when sending large amounts
6. **Do your own research** — if a token is "guaranteed" to 100x, it's a scam
7. **Use reputable, registered exchanges** in India (FIU-IND compliant)
8. **Be sceptical of investment advice from strangers** online or on social media

## If You've Been Scammed

- **Do not send more money** — scammers often call back as a "recovery agent" offering to help you recover funds (for a fee). They are the same scammer.
- Report to **cybercrime.gov.in** or call the National Helpline **1930**
- File a complaint with **CERT-In** (cert-in.org.in)
- Contact your exchange — they may be able to flag the destination address

<InfoBox type="warning">
  "Crypto recovery services" that claim they can get back lost crypto are almost always scams themselves. They charge upfront fees and deliver nothing. The irreversible nature of blockchain transactions means lost crypto is almost never actually recoverable.
</InfoBox>

## Key Takeaways

<InfoBox type="tip">
  - Crypto transactions are irreversible — scammers know this and exploit it
  - Never share your seed phrase with anyone for any reason
  - Guaranteed returns, time pressure, and unsolicited investment advice are red flags
  - Pig butchering (romance scams with crypto) is among the most financially devastating fraud types
  - Verify wallet addresses character by character before sending large amounts
  - Report scams to cybercrime.gov.in (helpline: 1930) and CERT-In
  - "Crypto recovery services" are almost always scams — lost crypto is rarely recoverable
</InfoBox>
