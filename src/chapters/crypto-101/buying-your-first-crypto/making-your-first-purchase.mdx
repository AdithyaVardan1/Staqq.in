# Making Your First Purchase

You've completed KYC. Now it's time to actually buy crypto. This chapter walks through the mechanics of your first trade.

<InfoBox type="important">
  This chapter is educational only. Nothing here is a recommendation to buy any specific asset. Crypto prices are highly volatile.
</InfoBox>

## Step 1: Deposit INR

Before buying, you need to fund your exchange account with Indian Rupees.

<InfoBox type="note">
  Some banks have blocked UPI payments to crypto exchanges. If your UPI fails, try IMPS or NEFT instead, or switch to a different bank account.
</InfoBox>

## Step 2: Choose What to Buy

For your first purchase, most people start with **Bitcoin (BTC)** or **Ethereum (ETH)** — the two largest, most liquid, and most established cryptocurrencies.

Avoid starting with small altcoins or meme coins until you understand the space better.

## Step 3: Market Order vs Limit Order

**Market Order** — buy immediately at the current market price
- Simple and fast
- You pay the current "ask" price
- Small slippage possible on low-volume pairs

**Limit Order** — set the price you want to buy at
- Only executes if the market reaches your price
- No slippage
- May never fill if price doesn't reach your target

For small purchases, a **market order** is fine.

## Step 4: Understanding What You're Buying

When you buy ₹1,000 of Bitcoin:
- You don't need to buy a whole Bitcoin (currently worth millions of rupees)
- You buy a **fraction** — Bitcoin is divisible to 8 decimal places
- At ₹60 lakh per BTC, ₹1,000 buys you ≈ 0.00000167 BTC (167 satoshis)

## Step 6: After You Buy

Once your purchase completes:
- Your crypto appears in your exchange wallet balance
- You can hold it there, sell it, or withdraw it to your personal wallet
- **Keep records** — you'll need them for tax filing

<InfoBox type="warning">
  Under India's Budget 2022 rules, crypto gains are taxed at a **flat 30%** (plus 4% cess = effectively 31.2%). Keep a record of every purchase date, amount, and price. This is your **cost basis** for tax calculation.
</InfoBox>

## When to Move Crypto Off the Exchange

If you plan to hold for months or years, consider withdrawing to a **personal wallet** (hardware wallet or non-custodial software wallet). This removes exchange risk (hacks, insolvency).

Keep in mind: withdrawing crypto has a fee, and you are then solely responsible for securing your keys.

## Key Takeaways

<InfoBox type="tip">
  - Deposit INR via UPI (fastest and free) before buying
  - Start with BTC or ETH — most liquid and established
  - Market orders are simplest; limit orders give price control
  - You buy fractions of coins — you don't need a full Bitcoin
  - Record every transaction for tax purposes — gains are taxed at 30% flat in India
</InfoBox>
