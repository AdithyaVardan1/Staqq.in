# KYC & Verification

Before you can buy crypto on any Indian exchange, you must complete KYC (Know Your Customer) verification. This is a legal requirement, not the exchange's choice.

<InfoBox type="important">
  This chapter is educational only. KYC requirements may change — always check the exchange's current requirements.
</InfoBox>

## Why is KYC Required?

India's crypto exchanges are now regulated under the **Prevention of Money Laundering Act (PMLA)** and are classified as **Reporting Entities** under the Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU-IND) framework.

This means they must:
- Verify the identity of every customer
- Report suspicious transactions to the government
- Maintain records of all transactions
- Comply with anti-money laundering (AML) standards

Without KYC, you cannot deposit INR, buy crypto, or withdraw funds above minimal limits.

- Proof of address (utility bill, bank statement)

## The KYC Process: Step by Step

1. **Create an account** — email ID and phone number
2. **Upload PAN** — front of your PAN card
3. **Upload Aadhaar** — front and back (some use DigiLocker integration)
4. **Selfie / video KYC** — a live photo or short video to verify it's really you
5. **Bank account linking** — add your bank account for INR transfers
6. **Verification** — exchanges typically verify within minutes to 24 hours

<InfoBox type="note">
  Your PAN is crucial — all your crypto gains are linked to it for tax purposes. The exchange reports to the Income Tax Department. Ensure the name on your PAN matches your bank account.
</InfoBox>

## Is Your Data Safe?

Exchanges are legally required to store KYC data securely. The data is used for:
- Identity verification
- Regulatory reporting (suspicious transaction reports to FIU)
- Tax-related information sharing with IT department (under Section 285BA)

Always use exchanges that store KYC data encrypted and have a clear privacy policy.

## 1% TDS and KYC

From July 2022, a **1% Tax Deducted at Source (TDS)** applies on crypto transactions above ₹50,000 per year (₹10,000 for certain cases). This TDS is automatically deducted by the exchange and reflected in your Form 26AS.

Your PAN is required for this — another reason KYC with a correct PAN is essential.

## Key Takeaways

<InfoBox type="tip">
  - KYC is a legal requirement under India's PMLA — not optional
  - You need PAN, Aadhaar, selfie, and bank account to complete KYC
  - Your PAN links all crypto activity to your tax record — ensure it's accurate
  - 1% TDS is deducted at source on qualifying transactions by the exchange
  - KYC data is stored by exchanges for regulatory compliance
</InfoBox>
