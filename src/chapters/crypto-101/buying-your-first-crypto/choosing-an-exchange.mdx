# Choosing an Exchange

To buy crypto in India, you need an account on a crypto exchange. Choosing the right one matters for your security, costs, and legal compliance.

<InfoBox type="important">
  This chapter is educational only. Always conduct your own research before using any financial platform. Mention of any exchange is not an endorsement.
</InfoBox>

## What is a Crypto Exchange?

A crypto exchange is a platform where you can:
- Convert Indian Rupees (INR) into cryptocurrency
- Trade one crypto for another
- Withdraw crypto to your personal wallet
- Sell crypto back to INR and withdraw to your bank

## Types of Exchanges

### Centralised Exchanges (CEX)
Run by a company. You create an account, complete KYC, and the exchange holds your funds (custodial). Easy for beginners.

### Decentralised Exchanges (DEX)
Smart contracts on the blockchain. No account, no KYC, connect your own wallet. Used for DeFi tokens and advanced trading.

For beginners in India, start with a **CEX**.

## FIU-Registered Indian Exchanges

India's Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU-IND) requires crypto businesses to register under the Prevention of Money Laundering Act (PMLA). Using a registered exchange is important for legal compliance.

## Comparing Fees

Most Indian exchanges charge a **maker/taker fee** model:
- **Maker** — placing a limit order that adds liquidity (usually lower fee)
- **Taker** — placing a market order that takes from existing liquidity (slightly higher)

Typical fees on Indian exchanges range from 0.1% to 0.5% per trade. On ₹10,000 of BTC, that is ₹10–₹50 per trade.

## Key Takeaways

<InfoBox type="tip">
  - Use a FIU-registered Indian exchange for legal compliance
  - CEXs (like CoinDCX, WazirX) are best for beginners
  - Compare fees, security practices, and coin selection before choosing
  - Never keep large amounts of crypto on any exchange for long periods — withdraw to your own wallet
</InfoBox>
