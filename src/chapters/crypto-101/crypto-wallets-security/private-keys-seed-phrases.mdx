# Private Keys & Seed Phrases

Your seed phrase is the master key to all your crypto. If someone gets it, your funds are gone. There is no customer support, no fraud department, no reversal.

<InfoBox type="important">
  This chapter is educational only. Never share your seed phrase or private key with anyone under any circumstances.
</InfoBox>

## Public Keys and Private Keys

Cryptography uses pairs of keys:

**Public Key → Your Address**
- Like your bank account number — safe to share
- People send crypto to your public address
- Example: `0x742d35Cc6634C0532925a3b844Bc454e4438f44e`

**Private Key → Your Signature**
- Like your PIN — never share this
- Proves you own the address
- Required to send crypto out
- A 256-bit number, usually shown as 64 hexadecimal characters

<InfoBox type="warning">
  Anyone who has your private key has complete control of your wallet. They can drain all funds instantly and irreversibly.
</InfoBox>

## What is a Seed Phrase?

Managing raw private keys (64-character strings) is impractical. The industry adopted **BIP-39**, a standard that converts a private key into 12 or 24 ordinary English words.

Example (never use this):
> `abandon ability able about above absent absorb abstract absurd abuse access accident`

This seed phrase:
- Is generated when you create a new wallet
- Can recreate your private keys on any compatible wallet
- Controls ALL accounts and all coins in that wallet
- Is the single most important thing to protect

## The 3-2-1 Backup Rule (Adapted for Crypto)

- **3** copies of your seed phrase
- **2** different storage media (paper + metal)
- **1** stored in a separate physical location (e.g., bank safe deposit box)

## What Happens If You Lose Your Seed Phrase?

If you lose your seed phrase AND access to your wallet (device lost, broken), your funds are **permanently inaccessible**. There is no recovery option.

Estimates suggest 3–4 million BTC (worth hundreds of billions of dollars) is permanently lost due to forgotten keys and phrases.

## Key Takeaways

<InfoBox type="tip">
  - Private keys prove ownership; public keys (addresses) receive crypto
  - A seed phrase (12 or 24 words) is the master backup of your private keys
  - Store your seed phrase offline — on paper or engraved metal, never digitally
  - Losing your seed phrase = permanently losing access to your funds
  - Anyone who asks for your seed phrase is attempting to steal your crypto
</InfoBox>
