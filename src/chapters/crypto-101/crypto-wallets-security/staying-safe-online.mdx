# Staying Safe Online

Crypto scams have cost users billions of dollars globally. Unlike traditional finance, there is no fraud protection, no chargeback, and no way to recover stolen funds. Prevention is the only protection.

<InfoBox type="important">
  This chapter is educational. Always verify independently before taking any financial action.
</InfoBox>

## The Most Common Crypto Scams

### 1. Phishing Attacks
Fake websites that look identical to real exchanges or wallets. You type your credentials or seed phrase — and hand it directly to thieves.

**How to spot it:** Check the URL carefully. `coindcx.com` is real. `co1ndcx.com` or `coindc-x.com` are fakes.

### 2. "Giveaway" Scams
"Send 0.1 BTC and receive 0.2 BTC back!" — Elon Musk, Tesla, or some celebrity is supposedly doubling your crypto. They never are.

**Reality:** This is always a scam. No legitimate entity gives away free crypto.

### 3. Fake Customer Support
Someone on Telegram, Twitter, or Reddit claims to be "WazirX support" or "MetaMask team" and asks for your seed phrase or private key to "fix your account."

**Reality:** Real support teams never ask for your seed phrase. Ever.

### 4. Rug Pulls
A new DeFi project or token launches with big promises. Developers hype it up, people invest, then the team disappears with all the money.

**Signs:** Anonymous team, no audit, unrealistic APY promises, no clear utility.

### 5. Pig Butchering (Romance Scams)
You meet someone online (dating app, social media). After weeks of building trust, they introduce you to a "crypto investment platform" with amazing returns. You invest, gains look real, you invest more — then the platform and the person vanish with everything.

<InfoBox type="warning">
  Pig butchering scams are devastatingly effective. They can run for months. Be suspicious of any romantic contact that eventually steers toward a "great crypto opportunity."
</InfoBox>

### 6. Pump-and-Dump
Coordinated groups buy a small-cap token, hype it heavily on Telegram/Twitter, price spikes, retail investors pile in, then the group sells everything and the price crashes.

## Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

Always enable 2FA on your exchange accounts. Use an **authenticator app** (Google Authenticator, Authy) — not SMS.

- SMS 2FA is vulnerable to SIM swapping (attackers convince your telecom to transfer your number)
- Authenticator apps generate codes locally, not transmitted over the network

## Due Diligence Before Investing

Before putting money into any project:

1. Is the team **doxxed** (publicly known and verifiable)?
2. Has the smart contract been **audited** by a reputable firm?
3. Is there a **clear use case** beyond speculation?
4. Is there genuine community discussion, or only paid promoters?
5. What is the token **vesting schedule** for the team?

## Key Takeaways

<InfoBox type="tip">
  - Bookmark official sites — never search or click links from DMs
  - Enable 2FA using an authenticator app, not SMS
  - Your seed phrase is never needed by any support team — requests are always scams
  - New tokens and DeFi projects with anonymous teams and no audits are high-risk
  - If a return sounds too good to be true in crypto, it is
</InfoBox>
