# Hot vs Cold Wallets

A crypto wallet doesn't actually store your cryptocurrency. Your coins live on the blockchain. What a wallet stores are your **private keys** — the passwords that prove ownership and allow you to sign transactions.

<InfoBox type="important">
  This chapter is for educational purposes only. Not financial advice. Always research wallet options thoroughly before use.
</InfoBox>

## The Golden Rule

> "Not your keys, not your coins."

If you don't control your private keys, you don't truly own your crypto. This is why understanding wallets is essential.

## Hot Wallets — Connected to the Internet

Hot wallets are software applications that store your private keys on a device connected to the internet.

**Types:**
- **Browser extensions** — MetaMask, Phantom
- **Mobile apps** — Trust Wallet, Coinbase Wallet
- **Exchange wallets** — WazirX, CoinDCX hold keys on your behalf (custodial)

**Pros:**
- Convenient for daily use and DeFi
- Free to use
- Easy to set up

**Cons:**
- Vulnerable to hacking, phishing, and malware
- If your device is compromised, your funds can be stolen

<InfoBox type="note">
  Exchange wallets are custodial — the exchange holds your keys. If the exchange is hacked or goes bankrupt (as FTX did in 2022), you may lose your funds.
</InfoBox>

## Cold Wallets — Offline Storage

Cold wallets store your private keys on a device that is never connected to the internet.

**Types:**
- **Hardware wallets** — Ledger Nano S/X, Trezor
- **Paper wallets** — private key written/printed on paper (old method, risky if damaged)
- **Air-gapped computers** — old laptop kept permanently offline

**Pros:**
- Virtually immune to online hacking
- Ideal for storing large amounts long-term

**Cons:**
- Cost (hardware wallets run ₹6,000–₹15,000+)
- Physical damage or loss can mean losing funds
- Less convenient for frequent trading

## Which Should You Use?

A sensible approach for most people:

- **Small amounts / frequent trading** → keep on a reputable exchange (custodial)
- **Medium amounts** → non-custodial hot wallet
- **Large amounts / long-term holding** → hardware wallet (cold storage)

<InfoBox type="tip">
  The crypto community's standard advice: keep only what you actively trade on exchanges. Move anything significant to a hardware wallet you control.
</InfoBox>

## Key Takeaways

<InfoBox type="tip">
  - Wallets store private keys, not coins — coins live on the blockchain
  - Hot wallets are internet-connected and convenient but less secure
  - Cold wallets are offline and much more secure for long-term storage
  - Custodial wallets (exchanges) are easy but you don't control your keys
  - For significant holdings, use a hardware wallet
</InfoBox>
