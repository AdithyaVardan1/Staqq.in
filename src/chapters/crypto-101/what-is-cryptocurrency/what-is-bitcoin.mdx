# What is Bitcoin

Bitcoin is the world's first cryptocurrency — a form of digital money that exists entirely online and operates without any bank, government, or central authority.

## The Problem Bitcoin Solved

Before Bitcoin, all digital payments required a trusted middleman — a bank, a payment company, or a government. This creates problems:

- Banks can freeze accounts
- Governments can devalue currency through inflation
- Transactions can be reversed or censored
- Fees are imposed by intermediaries

In 2008, an anonymous person (or group) using the name **Satoshi Nakamoto** published a 9-page whitepaper titled *"Bitcoin: A Peer-to-Peer Electronic Cash System."* It described a way to send money directly between two people — anywhere in the world — without a third party.

## How Bitcoin Works (Simply)

Bitcoin is a shared ledger (record book) that everyone can read but no one can secretly edit.

1. You want to send ₹5,000 worth of Bitcoin to a friend
2. You broadcast that transaction to a global network of computers
3. Those computers verify the transaction is valid (you have enough BTC, it hasn't already been spent)
4. The transaction is recorded permanently in a block on the blockchain
5. Your friend receives the Bitcoin — no bank involved

## Bitcoin's Price History

Bitcoin was worth virtually nothing when it launched in 2009. The first documented real-world purchase was 10,000 BTC for two pizzas in 2010. As of 2024, Bitcoin has traded above $70,000 per coin.

<InfoBox type="warning">
  Bitcoin's price is extremely volatile. It has dropped 80%+ multiple times in its history. Never invest money you cannot afford to lose entirely.
</InfoBox>

## Is Bitcoin Legal in India?

Yes. The Supreme Court of India struck down the RBI's 2018 banking ban on crypto in 2020. You can legally:
- Buy, hold, and sell Bitcoin on registered Indian exchanges
- Transfer Bitcoin to your own wallet
- Pay 30% tax on profits (Budget 2022)

Bitcoin is **not** legal tender in India — merchants are not required to accept it as payment.

## Key Takeaways

<InfoBox type="tip">
  - Bitcoin is decentralised digital money — no bank or government controls it
  - Only 21 million BTC will ever exist, making it scarce
  - Transactions are transparent, irreversible, and permissionless
  - Holding and trading Bitcoin is legal in India but profits are taxed at 30%
  - Bitcoin is highly volatile — price swings of 50–80% are not uncommon
</InfoBox>
