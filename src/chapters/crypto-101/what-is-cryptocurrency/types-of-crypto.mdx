# Types of Cryptocurrency

There are over 10,000 cryptocurrencies in existence. Most are not worth your time. Understanding the broad categories helps you cut through the noise.

<InfoBox type="important">
  This chapter is educational. It does not recommend any specific cryptocurrency for investment. All crypto assets carry significant risk of loss.
</InfoBox>

## 1. Bitcoin (BTC) — Digital Gold

Bitcoin is the original and largest cryptocurrency by market cap. Its primary use case today is as a **store of value** — a scarce asset that people hold to preserve purchasing power over time, similar to gold.

- Fixed supply: 21 million coins
- Oldest and most battle-tested network
- Most widely accepted

## 2. Ethereum (ETH) — The Programmable Blockchain

Ethereum is a platform for running decentralised applications (dApps). It introduced **smart contracts** — code that runs automatically on the blockchain without human intervention.

- Powers most of DeFi and NFTs
- Second largest by market cap
- ETH is used to pay "gas" (transaction fees) on the network

<InfoBox type="note">
  Bitcoin does one thing well: move value. Ethereum does many things: tokens, DeFi, NFTs, DAOs. Think of Bitcoin as gold and Ethereum as a programmable economy.
</InfoBox>

## 3. Stablecoins — Pegged to Fiat Currency

Stablecoins are designed to maintain a stable value, typically $1 USD.

Stablecoins are widely used for:
- Moving money between exchanges without converting to fiat
- Earning yield in DeFi
- Sending money internationally cheaply

<InfoBox type="warning">
  Stablecoins are not risk-free. TerraUSD (UST) collapsed in 2022, losing nearly all its value within days. Always understand what backs a stablecoin.
</InfoBox>

## 4. Altcoins — Everything Else

"Altcoin" means any cryptocurrency other than Bitcoin. This includes:

- **Layer 1 blockchains** competing with Ethereum: Solana, Avalanche, BNB Chain, Cardano
- **Layer 2 scaling solutions**: Polygon (MATIC), Arbitrum, Optimism
- **DeFi tokens**: UNI (Uniswap), AAVE, Compound
- **Infrastructure tokens**: Chainlink (LINK), The Graph (GRT)

## 5. Meme Coins — Speculation-Driven

Meme coins start as jokes but can reach billions in market cap purely on community hype.

## Key Takeaways

<InfoBox type="tip">
  - Bitcoin is digital gold — scarce, decentralised, store of value
  - Ethereum is a programmable platform that powers DeFi and NFTs
  - Stablecoins maintain a $1 peg and are used for stability and transfers
  - Altcoins cover everything else — from legitimate platforms to pure speculation
  - Meme coins carry extreme risk and have no fundamental value
</InfoBox>
