# Impermanent Loss

Impermanent loss (IL) is the most misunderstood concept in DeFi. It's the reason why providing liquidity to a volatile pair can leave you worse off than simply holding the tokens.

<InfoBox type="important">
  Educational content only. Impermanent loss can significantly reduce returns from liquidity provision. Not financial advice.
</InfoBox>

## What is Impermanent Loss?

When you provide liquidity to an AMM pool, the ratio of tokens in the pool shifts as trading happens. When you withdraw, you receive a different mix than you deposited — and this mix is often worth less than if you'd just held the original tokens.

It's called "impermanent" because if prices return to their original levels, the loss disappears. If you withdraw while prices are different, it becomes a **permanent** loss.

## A Concrete Example

You deposit into a 50/50 ETH/USDC pool when ETH = ₹2,00,000.

- You deposit: 1 ETH + ₹2,00,000 USDC = ₹4,00,000 total
- Your pool share: say 1%

Now ETH price rises to ₹4,00,000 (doubles).

The pool re-balances through arbitrage. Your 1% share now contains:
- ~0.707 ETH (less ETH than you put in)
- ~₹2,82,843 USDC (more USDC)
- Total value: ~₹5,65,685

**But if you had just held:**
- 1 ETH at ₹4,00,000 = ₹4,00,000
- ₹2,00,000 USDC = ₹2,00,000
- Total: ₹6,00,000

**Impermanent loss = ₹6,00,000 - ₹5,65,685 = ₹34,315 (about 5.7%)**

The higher the price divergence, the greater the impermanent loss.

## When Does IL Become Permanent?

IL is "impermanent" only as long as you keep your LP tokens. The moment you **withdraw your liquidity**, the loss is realised permanently — regardless of the word "impermanent."

## How to Mitigate IL

**1. Use stable pairs**
USDC/USDT or other pegged assets move together — minimal IL because prices stay near 1:1.

**2. Use concentrated liquidity wisely**
Uniswap V3 lets you set price ranges. Narrower range = more fees earned, but you exit the range (and stop earning) if price moves far.

**3. One-sided liquidity**
Some protocols (Bancor V3 historically, certain newer protocols) offer single-sided staking that hedges IL — but with caveats.

**4. Earn enough fees to offset IL**
High-volume pairs can generate enough trading fees to more than compensate for IL. BTC/ETH and ETH/USDC on Uniswap V3 with tight ranges often achieves this.

<InfoBox type="note">
  IL is not a bug — it's a feature of how AMMs work. It's the cost of providing liquidity. The question is whether fees (and any token rewards) are sufficient to compensate.
</InfoBox>

## Key Takeaways

<InfoBox type="tip">
  - Impermanent loss occurs when pooled token prices diverge after you deposit
  - The greater the price divergence, the larger the IL (up to 42.5% at 10x)
  - IL becomes permanent when you withdraw your liquidity
  - Stablecoin pairs have minimal IL — ideal for conservative yield
  - IL is offset by trading fees + token rewards; profitability depends on volume vs divergence
</InfoBox>
