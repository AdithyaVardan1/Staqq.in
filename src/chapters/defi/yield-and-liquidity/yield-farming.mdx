# Yield Farming

Yield farming means putting your crypto to work across DeFi protocols to earn the maximum possible return. At its peak in 2020–2021, some farms offered thousands of percent APY — most of which was unsustainable.

<InfoBox type="important">
  Educational content only. High APY in DeFi almost always comes with proportionally high risk. Not financial advice.
</InfoBox>

## What is Yield Farming?

Providing liquidity earns trading fees. But many protocols also distribute their **governance tokens** as additional incentives to attract liquidity. This is "liquidity mining."

Yield = Trading fees + Protocol token rewards

When protocol token prices are high and there's limited liquidity, APYs can appear astronomical. As more capital flows in and token prices stabilise, APYs normalise.

## The "DeFi Summer" of 2020

Compound Finance kicked off modern yield farming in June 2020 by distributing COMP tokens to users. Suddenly, lending and borrowing USDC on Compound yielded 20%+ APY — paid partly in COMP tokens.

The practice exploded. By August 2020, billions of dollars were rotating across protocols chasing the highest yields. This period was called "DeFi Summer."

## How It Works in Practice

**Example: Providing liquidity on Uniswap V3 + staking LP tokens**

1. Deposit ETH + USDC into a Uniswap V3 pool
2. Receive LP tokens
3. Stake LP tokens in the protocol's "farm" contract
4. Earn: trading fees (0.3%) + UNI governance tokens

**Example: Yield aggregator (Yearn Finance)**

1. Deposit USDC into Yearn
2. Yearn's "Strategy" automatically moves capital to the highest-yielding protocol
3. Compounds rewards back into the position
4. You just hold yUSDC tokens

<InfoBox type="warning">
  **"Mercenary capital"** is a real phenomenon — yield farmers move money from protocol to protocol chasing APY. When rewards dry up, liquidity leaves instantly, token prices crash, and remaining participants face losses. High APY is often a temporary incentive to bootstrap a protocol, not a permanent feature.
</InfoBox>

## Major Yield Aggregators

Rather than manually chasing yield across protocols, aggregators do it automatically:

- **Yearn Finance** — the original yield aggregator; focuses on stablecoin yield
- **Beefy Finance** — multi-chain, auto-compounds LP positions
- **Convex Finance** — maximises yield for Curve LP providers

## The Real Cost: Gas Fees and Complexity

On Ethereum mainnet, deploying and harvesting yield farming positions can cost $50–$200+ in gas. For small amounts, fees eat all returns.

Layer 2s and alternative chains (Solana, BNB Chain, Polygon) significantly reduce this barrier.

## Key Takeaways

<InfoBox type="tip">
  - Yield farming combines trading fees + protocol token rewards for total return
  - DeFi Summer (2020) kicked off the trend with Compound's COMP token distribution
  - APY above 20–30% almost always involves significant protocol token exposure and risk
  - Yields are not sustainable — they decline as more capital enters and token prices normalise
  - Aggregators like Yearn auto-compound and manage strategies automatically
</InfoBox>
