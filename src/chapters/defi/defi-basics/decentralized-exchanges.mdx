# Decentralised Exchanges (DEXes)

A DEX lets you swap one token for another directly from your wallet — no sign-up, no KYC, no company in the middle.

<InfoBox type="important">
  Educational content only. Not financial advice. DEX interactions involve gas fees and smart contract risk.
</InfoBox>

## How CEXes vs DEXes Differ

**Centralised Exchange (CEX):** You deposit funds to the exchange. The exchange maintains an internal order book. Trades settle in their database. You withdraw later.

**Decentralised Exchange (DEX):** You keep your funds in your wallet. Smart contracts handle swaps. Trades settle on the blockchain. No deposit or withdrawal needed.

## Two Types of DEXes

### 1. Order Book DEXes
Work like traditional exchanges — buyers post bids, sellers post asks. The DEX matches them.

**Examples:** dYdX (perpetuals), Serum (on Solana), Vertex

These work well for derivatives but are less common for spot trading on Ethereum due to gas costs.

### 2. Automated Market Makers (AMMs)
No order book. Instead, liquidity is pooled by users into smart contracts. A mathematical formula determines prices. This is how Uniswap works.

## How to Use Uniswap (Step by Step)

1. Go to app.uniswap.org
2. Connect your MetaMask wallet
3. Select tokens: e.g., ETH → USDC
4. Enter amount and review the rate + slippage tolerance
5. Click "Swap" and approve the transaction in MetaMask
6. Wait for the blockchain to confirm (~15–30 seconds)
7. USDC appears in your wallet

## Slippage

Slippage is the difference between the expected price and the actual execution price. It occurs because pool prices change between when you submit and when the transaction executes.

- **Low liquidity pools** have high slippage
- **Set a slippage tolerance** (e.g., 0.5%) — if slippage exceeds this, the transaction reverts
- **MEV bots** can sandwich your trade to extract value — high slippage tolerance worsens this

<InfoBox type="tip">
  For large trades, use aggregators like 1inch or Paraswap. They split your order across multiple DEXes to get the best price and minimal slippage.
</InfoBox>

## Key Takeaways

<InfoBox type="tip">
  - DEXes let you swap tokens from your own wallet — no KYC, no deposits
  - AMMs (Uniswap, Curve) use liquidity pools instead of order books
  - Major risk: smart contract vulnerabilities and slippage on low-liquidity pairs
  - Use aggregators (1inch) for large trades to minimise slippage
  - CEXes are easier; DEXes give you access to more tokens and full self-custody
</InfoBox>
