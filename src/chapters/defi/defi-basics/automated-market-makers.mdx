# Automated Market Makers (AMMs)

AMMs are the engine inside most DEXes. Instead of matching buyers with sellers in an order book, they use a mathematical formula and pooled liquidity to price and execute trades automatically.

<InfoBox type="important">
  Educational content only. Not financial advice.
</InfoBox>

## The Problem AMMs Solve

Traditional order books require:
- Active market makers to provide liquidity
- Enough buyers and sellers at the same time
- Off-chain infrastructure (expensive on blockchains)

For thousands of small token pairs with low trading volume, order books don't work. AMMs do.

## The Constant Product Formula

Uniswap V2 (and most basic AMMs) use this formula:

**x × y = k**

Where:
- `x` = amount of Token A in the pool
- `y` = amount of Token B in the pool
- `k` = a constant (never changes)

When you buy Token A, you remove some `x` from the pool and add `y` (paying with Token B). The formula ensures `k` stays constant — which means the price of A rises as you buy more of it.

**Example:**
- Pool: 100 ETH and 200,000 USDC (k = 20,000,000)
- You buy 10 ETH
- New ETH in pool: 90, so new USDC must be: 20,000,000 / 90 = 222,222 USDC
- Price paid: 222,222 - 200,000 = 22,222 USDC for 10 ETH
- Implied price: 2,222 USDC per ETH (vs 2,000 at start) — price rose as you bought

This price increase is called **price impact**. Larger trades in smaller pools = higher price impact.

## Concentrated Liquidity (Uniswap V3)

Uniswap V3 improved on this by letting liquidity providers concentrate their liquidity within specific price ranges.

**Benefit:** Capital efficiency — liquidity providers earn more fees for the same capital.

**Drawback:** More complex; LPs need to actively manage their price ranges.

## Stable AMMs (Curve Finance)

The constant product formula isn't efficient for assets that should trade near the same price (e.g., USDC/USDT). Curve uses a modified formula that keeps prices much more stable near 1:1, enabling near-zero slippage for stablecoin swaps.

## How Prices Stay Aligned with the Market

AMM prices would drift from global market prices without arbitrageurs. **Arbitrage bots** constantly:
1. Notice a price discrepancy (e.g., ETH is cheaper on Uniswap than Binance)
2. Buy ETH on Uniswap (pushing price up)
3. Sell on Binance (pushing price down there)
4. Profit the difference

This continuous arbitrage keeps AMM prices close to global market prices.

<InfoBox type="note">
  Arbitrage bots are also the reason fast blockchain confirmations matter. Stale prices create opportunities — and MEV (Maximal Extractable Value) bots compete to capture them.
</InfoBox>

## Key Takeaways

<InfoBox type="tip">
  - AMMs use pooled liquidity and a math formula instead of order books
  - The constant product formula (x*y=k) keeps pool value balanced as trades happen
  - Larger trades cause more price impact (slippage) in smaller pools
  - Uniswap V3 concentrated liquidity improves capital efficiency for LPs
  - Curve Finance specialises in stablecoin swaps with near-zero slippage
  - Arbitrageurs keep AMM prices aligned with global market prices
</InfoBox>
