# What is DeFi

DeFi — Decentralised Finance — is a global financial system built on blockchain smart contracts that anyone with an internet connection can access, without banks, brokers, or any intermediary.

<InfoBox type="important">
  Educational content only. DeFi involves significant risks including smart contract bugs, liquidation, and total loss of funds. Not financial advice.
</InfoBox>

## The Problem with Traditional Finance

Traditional finance (TradFi) requires:
- A bank account to store money
- KYC/documentation to participate
- Business hours and geographic restrictions
- A trusted third party to settle every transaction
- Permission to access services

Roughly 1.4 billion adults globally are "unbanked" — excluded from the financial system entirely.

## How DeFi Works

Every DeFi application is built on **smart contracts** — code that handles the financial logic automatically. No employee decides whether to give you a loan. No committee approves trades. The code runs 24/7/365 globally.

Key DeFi building blocks:
- **Tokens** (ERC-20): the money inside DeFi
- **Smart contracts**: the logic (interest rates, collateral rules, price calculations)
- **Oracles** (Chainlink): bring in real-world prices
- **Wallets** (MetaMask): your identity and access key

## TVL — Total Value Locked

The primary measure of DeFi's size is **TVL** (Total Value Locked) — the total dollar value of assets deposited across all DeFi protocols.

Peak TVL hit ~$180 billion in November 2021. After the 2022 bear market (Terra/Luna collapse, FTX collapse), TVL fell to ~$40B before recovering. Track live TVL at **DeFiLlama.com**.

## DeFi's Core Advantages

- **Permissionless** — no KYC, no account approval, no geographic restriction
- **Transparent** — all code and transactions are publicly auditable
- **Composable** — protocols can be combined like Lego bricks
- **Always on** — no market hours, no bank holidays

## DeFi's Core Risks

<InfoBox type="warning">
  DeFi has been the target of over $10 billion in hacks since 2020. Smart contract bugs, oracle manipulation, and governance attacks are real and have caused total loss of user funds in multiple protocols. Always use small amounts first, verify contract audits, and understand what you're interacting with.
</InfoBox>

- **Smart contract risk** — code bugs exploited
- **Liquidation risk** — leveraged positions wiped out
- **Stablecoin depeg** — UST lost 99%+ of value in 72 hours (May 2022)
- **Regulatory risk** — many DeFi protocols are in legal grey areas globally

## Key Takeaways

<InfoBox type="tip">
  - DeFi recreates financial services (lending, trading, savings) with smart contracts instead of banks
  - Permissionless and globally accessible — no account needed, just a wallet
  - TVL tracks the health and size of the DeFi ecosystem
  - Real risks: smart contract hacks, liquidations, stablecoin depegs, regulatory uncertainty
  - Only invest what you can afford to lose entirely
</InfoBox>
