# Liquidation Risks

Liquidation is DeFi's equivalent of a margin call — when your collateral value falls below a threshold, the protocol automatically seizes and sells it to repay your debt.

<InfoBox type="important">
  Educational content only. Liquidation risk can result in permanent loss of your collateral. Not financial advice.
</InfoBox>

## What Triggers Liquidation

Your position is liquidated when your **health factor drops below 1**.

Health factor = (Collateral Value × Liquidation Threshold) / Total Debt Value

When the price of your collateral drops OR the value of your debt rises (in a variable rate scenario), health factor decreases.

**Scenario:**
- Deposit: 2 ETH at ₹2,00,000 each = ₹4,00,000 collateral
- Borrowed: ₹2,40,000 USDC (60% LTV)
- Liquidation threshold: 82.5% LTV

Liquidation occurs when: ₹2,40,000 / collateral value = 82.5%
→ Collateral value must fall to ~₹2,91,000
→ This happens if ETH price falls from ₹2,00,000 to ~₹1,45,500

## The Liquidation Process

1. A **liquidation bot** monitors all positions on-chain continuously
2. When health factor < 1, the bot triggers liquidation
3. The bot repays some or all of your debt on your behalf
4. In return, the bot receives your collateral at a **discount** (the liquidation bonus)
5. Any remaining collateral after the debt is repaid stays with you

## How to Avoid Liquidation

1. **Borrow conservatively** — keep LTV well below the maximum (50% or less)
2. **Monitor your health factor** actively, especially in volatile markets
3. **Add collateral** when your position weakens — reduces LTV
4. **Repay debt** partially to improve health factor
5. **Use alerts** — Aave and third-party tools (DeBankAlert, Instadapp) can send notifications

<InfoBox type="warning">
  Crypto markets can move 20–30% in a single hour during extreme events. Positions that seem "safe" at 60% LTV can be liquidated within minutes if a major crash occurs (like the May 2021 or November 2022 crashes). Don't set and forget DeFi positions.
</InfoBox>

## Flash Loans — A Different Kind of Risk/Tool

Flash loans are a unique DeFi primitive: **borrow any amount of any asset with zero collateral, as long as you repay within the same transaction**.

If the repayment fails, the entire transaction reverts — the protocol never loses money.

**Legitimate uses:**
- Arbitrage between DEXes
- Collateral swaps (swap your collateral type in one transaction)
- Self-liquidation (liquidate your own position efficiently)

**Malicious uses:**
- Flash loan attacks have been used to manipulate oracle prices and drain DeFi protocols of hundreds of millions

Flash loans themselves aren't dangerous to users directly, but they are frequently used as tools in complex exploits.

## Key Takeaways

<InfoBox type="tip">
  - Liquidation is triggered automatically when health factor falls below 1
  - Liquidators receive your collateral at a 5–15%+ discount as a reward
  - Always borrow well below the maximum LTV to maintain a safety buffer
  - Monitor positions actively — crypto can move violently in minutes
  - Flash loans are zero-collateral loans that must be repaid within one transaction; often used in exploits
</InfoBox>
