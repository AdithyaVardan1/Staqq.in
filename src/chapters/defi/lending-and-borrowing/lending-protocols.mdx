# Lending Protocols

DeFi lending protocols let you earn interest on crypto assets you hold, or borrow assets against collateral — without a bank, credit check, or paperwork.

<InfoBox type="important">
  Educational content only. DeFi lending involves liquidation risk and smart contract risk. Not financial advice.
</InfoBox>

## How DeFi Lending Works

Traditional banks: you deposit money, the bank lends it to someone else at a higher rate, and keeps the spread.

DeFi lending protocols: smart contracts connect lenders and borrowers directly. The protocol manages interest rates algorithmically.

**Two sides of every lending pool:**

1. **Suppliers (lenders)** — deposit assets, earn interest
2. **Borrowers** — post collateral, borrow assets, pay interest

The interest rate adjusts automatically based on **utilisation rate** — how much of the supplied capital has been borrowed.

## APY vs APR

  ]}
/>

If an Aave pool shows 5% APY on USDC, a ₹1,00,000 deposit earns ~₹5,000 in a year — compounded continuously. Higher frequency compounding means APY > APR for the same nominal rate.

## Utilisation Rate and Interest Rate Model

As borrowing demand increases, the protocol raises rates to attract more suppliers:

- Low utilisation (10%) → Low supply rate, low borrow rate
- High utilisation (90%) → High supply rate, high borrow rate
- Near 100% utilisation → Very high rates (to incentivise repayment or new deposits)

## Supply Rate vs Borrow Rate

Borrow rates are always higher than supply rates. The difference (spread) goes to:
- **Protocol reserves** (safety fund)
- **Token holders** (in protocols that share revenue with governance stakers)

## Real Risk: Smart Contract Vulnerability

Aave and Compound have been running for years and are among the most audited protocols. However, no protocol is immune:

- Euler Finance (a Aave competitor) was exploited for ~$197 million in March 2023
- The attacker exploited a vulnerability in its donation feature

<InfoBox type="warning">
  Newer, less audited protocols offering higher yields carry significantly more smart contract risk. Higher yield = higher risk. Always check if a protocol has been audited.
</InfoBox>

## Key Takeaways

<InfoBox type="tip">
  - Lending protocols connect suppliers (earn interest) and borrowers (pay interest) via smart contracts
  - Aave and Compound are the most established protocols on Ethereum
  - Interest rates are set algorithmically based on utilisation rate
  - APY includes compounding; APR does not
  - Always verify audit status — newer protocols with high yields often carry higher smart contract risk
</InfoBox>
