# Market Cap & Volume

Understanding market cap and volume is the foundation of reading any crypto asset. These two numbers tell you most of what you need to know about a token's size and activity.

<InfoBox type="important">
  Educational content only. Market cap and volume do not predict future price performance. Not financial advice.
</InfoBox>

## Market Capitalisation

**Market Cap = Current Price × Circulating Supply**

If Bitcoin trades at ₹55,00,000 and 19.7 million BTC are in circulation:
Market Cap = ₹55,00,000 × 1,97,00,000 = ~₹1,08,35,000 crore (~$13 trillion equivalent)

Market cap tells you the **total value of all circulating tokens** at the current price.

## Fully Diluted Valuation (FDV)

**FDV = Current Price × Max Supply**

FDV shows what the market cap would be if all tokens (including locked, unvested, and future tokens) were in circulation today.

If a token has a circulating supply of 100 million but a max supply of 10 billion, the FDV is 100× the current market cap. Buying at current price = implicitly valuing the whole project at FDV.

<InfoBox type="warning">
  A large gap between market cap and FDV means significant future token dilution. Early investors and team members may dump their tokens as they unlock, suppressing price. Always check the FDV ratio before investing in newer tokens.
</InfoBox>

## Trading Volume

Volume is the total value of trades executed in a given period (usually 24 hours).

**High volume relative to market cap** → Active interest, easier to enter/exit positions

**Low volume relative to market cap** → Thin market, large trades will move price significantly, harder to exit

**Volume spike without price move** → Accumulation or distribution by large players (sometimes)

**Volume spike with price move** → Confirms the direction — institutional interest or panic selling

## Volume/Market Cap Ratio

A rough health check: divide 24h volume by market cap.

- > 10% = very active
- 1–10% = normal range
- < 1% = low interest, potential illiquidity

## Key Takeaways

<InfoBox type="tip">
  - Market cap = price × circulating supply — tells you the total value at current price
  - FDV = price × max supply — shows potential dilution from locked/unvested tokens
  - Large FDV-to-market-cap gap = significant future token unlock pressure
  - Market cap categories: mega, large, mid, small, micro — smaller = more volatile
  - Volume confirms or questions price moves — high volume = real interest
</InfoBox>
