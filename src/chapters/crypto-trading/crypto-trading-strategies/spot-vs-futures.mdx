# Spot vs Futures

Two ways to trade crypto — one you own the asset, one you're betting on its price. Understanding the difference is critical before placing any trade.

<InfoBox type="important">
  Educational content only. Futures trading can result in losses exceeding your initial capital. Not financial advice. Beginners should start with spot only.
</InfoBox>

## Spot Trading

In spot trading, you **buy and own the actual cryptocurrency**.

- Pay ₹1,00,000 for 0.002 BTC → you now own 0.002 BTC
- BTC rises to ₹60,00,000 per coin → your 0.002 BTC is now worth ₹1,20,000
- Sell → you made ₹20,000 profit (20%)
- Maximum loss: 100% (if BTC goes to zero)

Spot is straightforward. You hold what you bought.

## Futures Trading

In futures, you're entering a **contract** on the future price — without owning the underlying asset.

- Buy a long BTC futures contract worth ₹1,00,000 at 10x leverage
- Your margin: ₹10,000 (you're controlling ₹1,00,000 worth of BTC)
- BTC rises 10% → your position gains ₹10,000 → 100% return on margin
- BTC falls 10% → position loses ₹10,000 → 100% of margin gone (liquidated)

Leverage amplifies both gains AND losses proportionally.

## Types of Futures in Crypto

**Perpetual Futures (Perps):**
- No expiry date
- Most popular crypto futures product
- Funding rate mechanism keeps price near spot
- Available on: Binance, Bybit, dYdX, OKX

**Dated/Quarterly Futures:**
- Expire on a specific date (monthly or quarterly)
- Used by institutions for hedging
- Convergence to spot at expiry

<InfoBox type="warning">
  BTC commonly moves 5–10% in a single day. At 10x leverage, one bad day wipes your entire position. 95%+ of retail futures traders lose money according to exchange data. Avoid leverage entirely until you have significant experience with spot markets.
</InfoBox>

## Who Uses Futures and Why?

**Hedging:** A miner with 10 BTC can short BTC futures to lock in today's price and protect against a decline while they mine.

**Speculation:** Traders use leverage to amplify returns.

**Market neutral strategies:** Simultaneously long spot and short perps to capture funding rate.

## Key Takeaways

<InfoBox type="tip">
  - Spot: you own the asset; loss capped at 100%; suitable for all investors
  - Futures: you hold a contract; leveraged exposure; loss can exceed initial margin
  - Perpetual futures are the most traded crypto instrument — no expiry, funding every 8 hours
  - Higher leverage = liquidated faster by smaller price moves
  - Beginners should stick to spot trading — understand markets before using futures
</InfoBox>
