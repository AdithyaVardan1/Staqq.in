# Dollar-Cost Averaging (DCA)

Dollar-cost averaging is a strategy where you invest a fixed amount at regular intervals — regardless of price. It removes the nearly impossible task of timing the market.

<InfoBox type="important">
  Educational content only. DCA does not guarantee profits and does not protect against sustained decline. Not financial advice.
</InfoBox>

## The Problem It Solves

Nobody can consistently predict crypto prices. Even professional traders and analysts get timing wrong. DCA removes this pressure:

Instead of trying to buy the "perfect dip," you simply buy the same amount every week/month — catching all prices, high and low, and averaging your cost over time.

At ₹55,00,000 current price, your position is worth: 0.0009247 × 55,00,000 = ₹50,858

Gain: ₹10,858 on ₹40,000 invested (27%) — without needing to call the market bottom.

## DCA vs Lump Sum

<Table
  headers={["Approach", "When It Wins", "Risk"]}
  rows={[
    ["Lump Sum", "When price trends upward from your entry", "Full exposure from day 1; bad timing = large loss"],
    ["DCA", "In volatile or downtrending markets", "You keep buying if asset permanently declines; slower upside in strong bull runs"],

/>

Historically in Bitcoin, **lump sum outperforms DCA** over most long periods simply because Bitcoin's long-term trend is up. But psychologically and practically, DCA is easier for most people.

<InfoBox type="note">
  In India, many crypto exchanges offer recurring buy features — you can set up automatic weekly or monthly purchases of a fixed INR amount. This fully automates DCA. Check WazirX, CoinDCX, or CoinSwitch for this feature.
</InfoBox>

## DCA for Indian Investors: Tax Considerations

Each DCA purchase creates a separate **cost basis entry**. Keeping records is essential:

- Buy ₹5,000 of BTC every week for a year = 52 separate purchase records
- When you sell, you need to know each purchase date and cost for tax calculation
- India taxes crypto gains at 30% flat (plus 4% cess)

**Tip:** Use a portfolio tracker (Koinly, CoinTracker) that auto-imports from Indian exchanges and generates a tax report.

## DCA and Bear Markets

DCA is most powerful during bear markets — you buy more units at lower prices. An investor who DCA'd through the 2022 crypto bear market (Bitcoin fell from $68k to $16k) at the lows was accumulating BTC at much lower average costs, positioning well for the 2023–2024 recovery.

<InfoBox type="warning">
  DCA is not a guarantee of profit. If an asset goes to zero (like many altcoins have), consistent DCA results in a 100% loss. DCA works best for assets with strong long-term fundamentals — not meme coins or speculative projects.
</InfoBox>

## Key Takeaways

<InfoBox type="tip">
  - DCA: invest a fixed amount at regular intervals regardless of price
  - Reduces timing risk — you automatically buy more units when prices are lower
  - Most effective for assets with strong long-term fundamentals (BTC, ETH)
  - Each purchase is a separate tax lot — keep records for India's 30% crypto tax
  - Indian exchanges offer automated recurring buy features to simplify DCA
</InfoBox>
