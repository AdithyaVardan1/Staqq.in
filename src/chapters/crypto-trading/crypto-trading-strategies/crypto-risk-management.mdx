# Risk Management in Crypto

Crypto is one of the most volatile asset classes ever created. Proper risk management is not optional — it is the difference between staying in the game and blowing up your account.

<InfoBox type="important">
  Educational content only. Risk management does not guarantee against losses. Not financial advice.
</InfoBox>

## The First Rule

> Never invest more than you can afford to lose entirely.

This is not a cliché. Bitcoin has declined 80%+ four times in its history. Most altcoins decline 90–99% in bear markets and never recover. Plan your crypto position sizing assuming the worst case is zero.

## Position Sizing Within Crypto

Even within your crypto allocation, diversify:

- **Core (50–60%):** Bitcoin and Ethereum — most liquid, most established
- **Satellite (30–40%):** Established large-cap altcoins (Solana, BNB, etc.)
- **Speculative (10–20% max):** Smaller caps, DeFi tokens, new projects

The speculative portion should be money you're prepared to see go to zero.

## Stop Losses in Crypto

Stop losses work differently in crypto than stocks:

**On CEXes:** You can set stop-loss orders. When price hits your stop, a market or limit order triggers.

**Challenges:**
- Crypto can gap down 20–30% in minutes, executing your stop at a much worse price
- Exchanges have "wick hunting" — temporary price spikes that trigger stops before reversing
- During liquidation cascades, stops execute at severe slippage

**Alternative approach:** Instead of tight stop-losses, many crypto investors use **position sizing** — only invest what you'd be comfortable losing entirely, then ride the volatility.

## India-Specific: Tax Impact on Risk Management

India's 30% flat tax on crypto profits creates a unique risk management consideration:

- **No loss offset:** Crypto losses cannot be set off against other income or other crypto gains
- **No carry-forward (between different VDAs):** A loss on BTC cannot offset a gain on ETH
- This means tax is charged on every gain while losses provide no tax benefit

<InfoBox type="warning">
  Under India's current tax rules, if you make ₹1,00,000 profit on Bitcoin but ₹50,000 loss on an altcoin, you still pay 30% on the ₹1,00,000 profit. The loss provides no tax benefit. This makes diversifying into riskier altcoins even more costly in India from a tax perspective.
</InfoBox>

## The 1% Rule (Adapted for Crypto)

Professional traders often risk no more than 1–2% of their total portfolio on a single trade.

Example: ₹5,00,000 crypto portfolio → maximum risk per trade = ₹5,000–₹10,000

For most retail investors in crypto, this means keeping positions appropriately sized so a single bad trade doesn't devastate the portfolio.

## Emotional Risk Management

The biggest risk in crypto is yourself:

- **FOMO (Fear of Missing Out):** Buying tops because price is surging
- **FUD (Fear, Uncertainty, Doubt):** Panic selling bottoms
- **Overtrading:** Transaction costs and taxes erode returns
- **Anchoring:** Refusing to cut a loss because you "know it will come back"

Having a written plan before entering any position helps remove emotional decision-making.

## Key Takeaways

<InfoBox type="tip">
  - Never invest more than you can afford to lose entirely — crypto can and does go to zero
  - Limit total crypto allocation based on your risk tolerance (typically 1–30% of portfolio)
  - Core (BTC/ETH) + satellite (large altcoins) + small speculative allocation
  - India's tax rules: gains taxed at 30% flat; losses cannot offset other gains
  - Manage emotional risk with a written plan; avoid FOMO buys and panic sells
</InfoBox>
