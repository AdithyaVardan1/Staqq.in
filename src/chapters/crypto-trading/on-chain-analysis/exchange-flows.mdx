# Exchange Flows

Exchange flows track how much crypto is moving onto and off of exchanges. This is one of the most actionable on-chain metrics for understanding buying and selling pressure.

<InfoBox type="important">
  Educational content only. On-chain metrics are indicators, not certainties. Not financial advice.
</InfoBox>

## The Core Idea

Crypto held on exchanges is available for immediate sale. Crypto held in private wallets (cold storage) is not readily tradeable.

- **High exchange reserves** → More potential selling supply
- **Low exchange reserves** → Less available supply, potentially bullish
- **Increasing reserves** → People moving crypto to sell
- **Decreasing reserves** → People withdrawing to hold

## Exchange Inflow vs Outflow

**Net Exchange Flow = Inflows - Outflows**

## Bitcoin Exchange Reserves as a Macro Signal

Bitcoin exchange reserves have been in a long-term downtrend since 2020. This coincides with:
- Institutional accumulation (moving to custodians, not exchanges)
- Growing ETF holdings (BlackRock, Fidelity holding BTC off public exchanges)
- "Not your keys, not your coins" culture

Lower exchange reserves reduce the available float, potentially making prices more sensitive to demand.

<InfoBox type="note">
  The 2022 FTX collapse caused a massive outflow from exchanges as users panic-withdrew funds (after seeing Celsius and Voyager collapse earlier that year). This was actually bullish for exchange reserve metrics — but happened for bearish reasons. Context matters.
</InfoBox>

## Stablecoin Reserves on Exchanges

Tracking stablecoins (USDC, USDT) on exchanges shows "dry powder" — capital ready to buy.

- **Rising stablecoin inflows to exchanges** → Potential buying pressure incoming
- **High stablecoin reserves on exchange** → Buy walls being built
- **Stablecoin supply ratio (SSR)** — Bitcoin market cap divided by stablecoin supply; low SSR = lots of buying power relative to BTC size

## Tools for Exchange Flow Analysis

- **Glassnode** — the gold standard; tracks exchange reserves for BTC, ETH, and stablecoins
- **CryptoQuant** — exchange-specific inflow/outflow, miner flows
- **Nansen** — labelled exchange wallets for real-time tracking

## Real Example: 2022 Bear Market

In May 2022, Luna/UST collapsed, triggering panic. Exchange inflows for BTC and ETH spiked massively — people moving assets to exchanges to sell. This preceded the next leg down from ~$38k to $25k.

In January 2023, exchange reserves hit multi-year lows. Shortly after, BTC began its recovery rally from $16k to $30k.

## Key Takeaways

<InfoBox type="tip">
  - Exchange flows track crypto moving on/off trading platforms
  - High inflows = potential selling pressure; high outflows = accumulation
  - Bitcoin exchange reserves declining long-term = reducing available supply
  - Stablecoin reserves on exchanges = "dry powder" ready to buy
  - Use Glassnode or CryptoQuant for exchange flow data; interpret with broader context
</InfoBox>
