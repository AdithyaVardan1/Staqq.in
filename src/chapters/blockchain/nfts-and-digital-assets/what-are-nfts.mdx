# What are NFTs

NFT stands for **Non-Fungible Token**. Understanding the word "fungible" is the key to understanding NFTs.

<InfoBox type="important">
  Educational content only. The NFT market is highly speculative. Not financial advice.
</InfoBox>

## Fungible vs Non-Fungible

**Fungible** means interchangeable. One ₹100 note is worth exactly the same as any other ₹100 note. One Bitcoin equals any other Bitcoin. They are fungible.

**Non-fungible** means unique. The original Mona Lisa is not interchangeable with a print of it. Your specific plot of land is not the same as a similar plot nearby. They are non-fungible.

An NFT is a **unique token on the blockchain**. While you can have millions of identical USDT tokens, each NFT has a distinct identity and provenance recorded permanently on-chain.

## What an NFT Actually Is

An NFT is a record on the blockchain that says:
- Token ID: #3429
- Contract address: `0xBC4CA...` (the Bored Ape Yacht Club collection)
- Owner: `0x742d35...` (your wallet address)

What the NFT typically does **not** store on-chain: the actual image or file. Most NFTs store a URL pointing to a file hosted elsewhere (usually IPFS or a centralised server).

<InfoBox type="warning">
  When you buy an NFT, you are buying the blockchain entry that says you own a token ID. You usually do not own the copyright to the artwork, the ability to commercially exploit it, or even the underlying file — unless explicitly stated in the contract terms.
</InfoBox>

## How NFT Ownership Works

1. A creator deploys a smart contract (e.g., ERC-721)
2. They mint tokens: each gets a unique ID, metadata, and a link to the asset
3. Someone buys token #3429 — the blockchain records their wallet as owner
4. They can sell it: ownership transfers to the new buyer's wallet
5. All ownership history is permanently public on the blockchain

## The NFT Boom and Correction

2021 was the peak of the NFT frenzy:
- Beeple's digital art sold for $69 million at Christie's auction house
- Bored Ape Yacht Club (BAYC) apes sold for hundreds of ETH (millions of dollars)
- Total NFT trading volume exceeded $25 billion in 2021

By 2022–2023, most NFT prices fell 90–99%+. Speculation drove much of the 2021 boom.

<InfoBox type="note">
  The speculative crash doesn't mean NFTs have no utility. The technology has genuine use cases — event tickets, gaming assets, digital identity, real-world asset tokenisation. But 2021 valuations for JPEGs were largely speculative.
</InfoBox>

## Key Takeaways

<InfoBox type="tip">
  - Non-fungible = unique; each NFT is a distinct token with its own identity
  - NFTs are blockchain records of ownership — the actual file is usually stored elsewhere
  - Buying an NFT typically does not give you copyright or commercial rights to the asset
  - NFT technology has genuine use cases beyond speculative art: gaming, tickets, identity
  - The 2021 NFT boom was largely speculative; most prices crashed significantly
</InfoBox>
