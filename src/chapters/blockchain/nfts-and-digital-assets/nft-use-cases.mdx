# NFT Use Cases

Beyond the speculative art boom of 2021, NFTs have practical applications that may outlast the hype cycle.

<InfoBox type="important">
  Educational content only. NFT markets are highly speculative. Not financial advice.
</InfoBox>

## 1. Digital Art and Collectibles

The original mainstream NFT use case. Artists can:
- Sell digital art with **verifiable scarcity** (limited editions of 10, or 1-of-1)
- Receive **royalties** automatically on secondary sales (though this is increasingly optional on marketplaces)
- Sell directly to collectors without galleries or auction houses

**Reality check:** The 2021–2022 boom was heavily speculative. Most NFT art collections have lost 90–99%+ of peak value. Genuine digital art with strong artist reputation holds value better.

## 2. Gaming and Virtual Assets

This is arguably the most durable use case. NFTs allow:

- **True ownership** of in-game items (sword, skin, character)
- Items that can be **traded** outside the game ecosystem
- Interoperability (in theory) across multiple games

**Examples:** Gods Unchained (trading card game with tradeable cards), Illuvium, Star Atlas

<InfoBox type="note">
  Most gaming NFT projects over-promised on interoperability. Moving an item between games requires all games to explicitly support it — still rare in practice.
</InfoBox>

## 3. Event Tickets

NFT tickets solve the ticket scalping and fraud problem:
- Provably authentic (blockchain verifiable)
- Transferable but the artist/venue can take a cut on resales
- Can unlock perks (backstage access, exclusive merch)

**Examples:** GET Protocol, GUTS Tickets, Coachella lifetime passes as NFTs

## 4. Real-World Asset (RWA) Tokenisation

NFTs can represent ownership of physical assets:
- **Real estate:** fractional ownership of a property
- **Luxury goods:** Rolex, handbag, fine wine with on-chain provenance
- **Art:** tokenised ownership of physical paintings

This is a growing area with significant institutional interest in 2024–2025.

## 5. Identity and Credentials

- **Soulbound tokens (SBTs)** — non-transferable NFTs representing credentials, memberships, or achievements
- Academic degrees, certifications, work history on-chain
- DAO membership and reputation scores

## 6. Music and Entertainment

- Musicians selling albums/songs directly as NFTs to fans
- Royal.io: buy fractions of song royalties as NFTs
- Exclusive fan communities gated by NFT ownership

## Key Takeaways

<InfoBox type="tip">
  - NFTs have use cases beyond speculative art: gaming, tickets, RWA, identity, music
  - Gaming assets with true on-chain ownership may be the most durable use case
  - RWA tokenisation (real estate, luxury goods) is attracting institutional interest
  - Soulbound tokens could revolutionise digital identity and credentials
  - The 2021 speculation has faded, but the underlying technology has genuine utility
</InfoBox>
