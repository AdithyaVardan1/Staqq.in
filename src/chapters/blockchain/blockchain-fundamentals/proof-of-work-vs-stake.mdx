# Proof of Work vs Proof of Stake

These are the two most important consensus mechanisms in crypto. Understanding their trade-offs explains many debates in the industry.

<InfoBox type="important">
  This chapter is educational only. Not financial advice.
</InfoBox>

## Proof of Work (PoW)

Used by Bitcoin since 2009. Miners compete to solve a computationally intensive puzzle. The first to solve it earns the right to add the next block and receives a reward.

**How it works:**
1. You take the block's data and a random number called a **nonce**
2. You run it through a hash function (SHA-256 for Bitcoin)
3. The output must start with a certain number of zeros (set by difficulty)
4. If your hash doesn't qualify, change the nonce and try again
5. Globally, Bitcoin miners make trillions of attempts per second

**Security:** An attacker would need to out-compute the entire rest of the network (51% attack). Bitcoin's hash rate is so massive this is practically impossible.

**Downside:** Enormous electricity consumption. Bitcoin mining consumes roughly as much electricity as a medium-sized country.

## Proof of Stake (PoS)

Used by Ethereum (since "The Merge" in September 2022), Cardano, Solana, and most newer blockchains. Instead of computing power, validators put up **stake** (locked crypto) as collateral.

**How it works:**
1. Validators lock up (stake) crypto as collateral (e.g., 32 ETH for Ethereum)
2. The network randomly selects a validator to propose the next block
3. Other validators attest (verify) the block
4. Correct validators earn rewards; dishonest validators lose stake (**slashing**)

**Security:** Attacking the network requires owning 33%+ of all staked ETH — worth tens of billions of dollars. Economic punishment (slashing) deters bad behaviour.

**Advantage:** ~99.95% less energy than PoW. Ethereum's energy consumption dropped by over 99% after The Merge.

## The Merge — Ethereum's Switch

In September 2022, Ethereum switched from PoW to PoS in an event called **The Merge**. This was one of the most significant technical events in crypto history — the equivalent of changing an airplane's engine mid-flight.

The Merge reduced Ethereum's energy consumption by over 99.95% overnight.

<InfoBox type="note">
  Neither PoW nor PoS is universally "better." Bitcoin maximalists argue PoW is more battle-tested and decentralised. Ethereum advocates argue PoS is more efficient and scalable. Both have trade-offs.
</InfoBox>

## Key Takeaways

<InfoBox type="tip">
  - PoW (Bitcoin) uses energy/computation as the security mechanism
  - PoS (Ethereum, Cardano) uses locked collateral with slashing penalties
  - PoS is ~99.95% more energy-efficient than PoW
  - Ethereum switched from PoW to PoS in September 2022 (The Merge)
  - Both have proven security but make different decentralisation trade-offs
</InfoBox>
